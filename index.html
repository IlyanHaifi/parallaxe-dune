About the film

Dune is an epic science-fiction film that brings Frank Herbert’s legendary novel to life with striking visuals, deep world-building, and an emotional story about destiny, power, and survival. Set far in the future, the film follows Paul Atreides, a young man born into a powerful noble family. When Paul’s family is entrusted with control of the desert planet Arrakis—the only source of the universe’s most valuable substance, “spice”—they become targets in a dangerous political struggle. Arrakis is a harsh world ruled by extreme conditions, mysterious desert tribes, and colossal sandworms that roam beneath the sand. As betrayal and conflict erupt, Paul must confront visions of his future and discover the role he is meant to play in the fate of the planet and its people. Blending breathtaking landscapes, immersive sound, and a thoughtful exploration of themes like prophecy, ecology, and human ambition, Dune delivers a cinematic journey that feels both intimate and monumental. It stands as a powerful reimagining of a classic story, inviting audiences into a world as beautiful as it is unforgiving.
